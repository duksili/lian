# Product Charter

## Product statement

LIAN is a private personal practice-and-research workstation. It helps one person collect consistent longitudinal data about practice, movement, sleep, subjective state, context, and repeatable assessments; plan intended activities and personal determinations; and review patterns without making unjustified causal claims.

## Primary user outcome

After several months of ordinary use, the user can answer questions such as:

- What practice did I actually do, and how consistently?
- Which days or weeks had unusually good or poor energy, focus, calm, tension, recovery, sleep, or performance?
- Are there repeatable associations between practice exposure and later outcomes?
- Which patterns are strong enough to justify a structured personal protocol?
- What life context may explain a change in results?

## Product posture

The application is:

- private and local-first;
- calm rather than motivational;
- evidence-aware rather than pseudo-scientific;
- designed for years of use;
- useful as a journal before it becomes useful as a research system.

The application is not intended to diagnose health conditions, prescribe treatment, provide medical advice, assess moral worth, or replace professional care.

## Essential capabilities

1. Log completed practices, activity, life events, and context.
2. Record a short daily state check-in.
3. Track Five Precepts privately in a simple daily form.
4. Create and review voluntary personal determinations without turning them into moral scores.
5. Plan future activity and commitments on a calendar.
6. Run scheduled repeated assessments.
7. Store raw assessment data and protocol metadata.
8. Send restrained, configurable reminders.
9. Review daily, weekly, monthly, and research-oriented patterns.
10. Export and back up all user-owned data.
11. Support structured research protocols after baseline data exists.

## Quality bar

The app succeeds when it is trustworthy enough that the user can rely on its timeline, data provenance, and review output years later. It must also feel exceptionally polished, calm, and coherent as a desktop product; visual refinement is a delivery requirement, not decorative surplus. It does not succeed merely because it has many charts or a visually impressive dashboard.
